# Agonic Actor Rechartering

## Purpose

Wave I exists because Agon is not merely an extension of the current agent layer. Agon audits the current layer.

Before `Agents-of-Abyss` can open a lawful arena, `aoa-agents` must recharter its role-bearing actors into actor forms that can be read by that future arena. The current public profiles remain useful: they describe who acts, what role contract they carry, and which neighboring layers they must not absorb. They do not yet describe full subject-bearing actor form.

This document lands the first Agon-facing answer: an existing profile can survive as an Agon-ready actor only through reviewed companion surfaces.

## Recharter rule

The base profile is still the role contract. The companion formation surfaces are the Agon-readiness contract.

A rechartered agonic actor must have:

- `agent_kind_v1`
- `agent_subjectivity_v1`
- `agent_office_overlay_v1`
- `agent_arena_eligibility_v1`
- `agent_resistance_revision_v1`

Without these, the actor remains legacy-only.

## What this recharter does

It gives the existing five role homes their first battle-facing form:

- `architect` becomes a boundary-keeper / tracer-facing actor.
- `coder` becomes a bounded executor-facing actor.
- `reviewer` becomes a verifier / skeptic-facing actor.
- `evaluator` becomes an arbiter / judge-candidate actor.
- `memory-keeper` becomes a chronicler / witness-facing actor.

These are not new public profile families. They are office overlays on current role homes.

## What this recharter does not do

This wave does not create:

- arena sessions
- sealed commits
- challenge packets
- contradiction ledgers
- verdict logic
- scar ledgers
- retention checks
- summon protocol
- closure protocol
- Tree-of-Sophia promotion

Those belong to future center-owned Agon protocol work and sibling layers.

## One-line law

`aoa-agents` owns actor form under Agon scrutiny, not Agon law itself.
